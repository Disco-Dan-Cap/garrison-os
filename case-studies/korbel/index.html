03 — The short version

The short version

In February 2023 — when generative AI meant DALL·E 2 and a young Midjourney — I rebranded Korbel as a 1960s alpine resort pour: new label system, travel-poster campaign, packaging, even a champagne-tower hero shot. The direction held up. The tools didn't. Faces melted, signage came out as gibberish, and the bottle changed shape between frames. In 2026 I went back with modern models and re-executed the same art direction, shot for shot. Both versions live on this page, because the pairing is the story: the constant is the direction; the tools just caught up.

04 — The brand

Chalet Elixir, the system

Korbel's actual label whispers grocery store. The concept reframes it as a destination: a triangular pine-and-peak motif, a cream ground, persimmon and pine pulled from 1960s travel posters, and a script that belongs on a chairlift ticket. The 2023 label system below was hand-built in Illustrator — the part of the project no model could do then, and the part I'd still do by hand today.

05 — Then & now

Same direction, three years of tools

The champagne tower

The 2023 file is literally named "a video of a man climbing a tower of champagne glasses." I was prompting for video before video existed — the tool handed back a blurry still. The 2026 frame holds the pour, the hands, and the poster texture at the same time.

The bottle

The 2026 label drops the badge that floated over the 2023 artwork — the mountain painting is the label now, with the script beneath it. Wordmarks stay vector; they're composited, never generated.

The campaign world

06 — Packaging

Follow the arrows

The 2026 pass earned a packaging idea the 2023 tools couldn't draw: gift boxes shaped like ski-trail directional signs — routed white borders, black-diamond markers, an arrow that points somewhere worth going. Two tiers: a matte premium box, and a special-edition enameled tin with a vintage skier panel.

For contrast, the 2023 packaging pass: boxes and bottles, February 2023 — right palette, wandering geometry.

07 — What this proves

The constant is the direction

Anyone can prompt a pretty picture in 2026. The point of this page is the 2023 column: the palette, the era, the label system, and the shot list were all locked when the tools could barely hold a face together — and they were strong enough to survive three model generations unchanged. Art direction is the durable skill; the renderer is a line item. Where geometry must be perfect — wordmarks, label vectors, flat patterns — it's still drawn, not generated.
